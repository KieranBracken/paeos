## Why not RATIFY

The court proved a formal contradiction (F7: amendments cannot satisfy the lattice ordering their own evolution loop requires), a forgeable foundation (F10: I1 gates on evidence any agent can fabricate), an instability with no convergence guarantee (F8: the I2×I9 rebase treadmill), and two missing subsystems (economics — F13/F16; tenancy — H10, which contradicts the stated multi-project roadmap). A constitution containing a proven contradiction cannot be ratified as written.

## Why not REJECT or REJECT WITH MAJOR AMENDMENTS

Three reasons, in ascending strength:

1. **Every demonstrated defect admits a bounded amendment.** No primitive required forcible removal; no invariant required abandonment; the skeleton required no rework. The worst defects fix with one lattice state (A-06), one binding-mechanism substitution (A-07), one syscall contract clause (A-09), and one conservation law (A-12).
2. **The corpus repeatedly contained its own fixes, unused.** The Evidence provenance field anticipated A-08 but no invariant consumed it; the "verification seams" heuristic anticipated A-07 but was never elevated to an admission rule; PAEOS-1 §8 was already routing amendments as goals, anticipating A-02; genesis's imported scars anticipated A-18. This pattern is diagnostic: the design is **under-constrained, not wrong** — over-derived in prose, under-specified in enforcement. That is precisely the defect class PAEOS-4 (schemas and contracts) exists to close, meaning the roadmap already points at the cure.
3. **The independence test converged.** Re-deriving from PAEOS-0's axioms alone, without the corpus, reproduces the same skeleton — store, evidence-with-expiry, ephemeral stanced agents, tournaments — and the *only* two places the independent derivation diverges (justification graphs; an explicit economics layer) are the same two gaps the adversarial attacks found by an unrelated method. Two independent methods locating identical defects in an otherwise-convergent structure is the strongest available evidence that the skeleton is forced by the axioms and the defects are exhaustively enumerated rather than the first of many.

## What survived attack outright

The verifiability × reversibility router (no superior ceremony basis was constructible); the evolution loop's scar/falsifier/pruning discipline (with A-13/A-18 patches); the narrow waist and null-adapter conformance strategy; the founder ABI and clean-intent invariant (with H2's honesty clause); the genesis-authority bootstrap with scheduled expiry; the demand-paging rule; Skills-as-Policy. These include every element the corpus claims as novel. **The novelty is real and it held.**

## What the ratified kernel looks like after amendment

Four primitives (Artifact, Goal, Evidence, Policy) · ten axioms + one new conservation law + two derived guarantees · three stances × four domains · three primitive syscalls + one optimization point · evidence as a justification graph with adapter-signed execution records · namespaced policy for multi-tenancy · probationary integration for policy changes.

Smaller than what was submitted, and stronger — which is what a constitution should look like after meeting its court.
