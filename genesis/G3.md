# G3 — Validator Registered; First Evidence

## Forcing clause
PAEOS-3 SVK-3 (the validator is the first executable check; mechanical enforcement or the
constitution is prose — A6) · PAEOS-4 §11 WRITE validation · PAEOS-4.5 §1.4.

## Writes
No new *founding* objects — this batch establishes that the validator runs and produces
the store's **first Evidence**: `Evidence(class=execution)` recording that the validator
executed over the G1+G2 batch and that the store conforms (schemas parse, the GenesisRecord
is well-formed, no invariant is violated). Per A-09 this Evidence is emitted by the EXEC
path and adapter-signed — the first exercise of the signing trust root registered in G1.

## Inputs
The committed G1+G2 store state; the validator registry (implementation: I-VAL, backlog Wave 2).

## Exit evidence
A signed `execution` Evidence object exists asserting store conformance. This is the moment
the extra-constitutional genesis period begins to close (PAEOS-3 S0): the constitution is
now subject to a running check rather than founder assertion.

## Note
G3 is where "the validator validates the validator's own admission of the constitution"
first happens. It is not circular in the dangerous sense: the validator *code* is host-
toolchain (genesis authority, cross-compiled), and its trustworthiness is a stated
assumption (PAEOS-5.5 Ω-13, the non-Byzantine-kernel assumption) whose long-run defense is
dual-adapter differential conformance, not self-certification.
